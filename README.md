## Thesis

Spencer-Brown's Cancellation rule $[\bullet] \to \emptyset$ is used as a load-bearing primitive throughout the 29-schisms framework. But its own philosophical content — what "crossing a boundary" means, how it relates to nothingness/void, whether it implies anti-realism about boundaries, whether "boundary = measurement" is justified — was never rigorously defended on its own terms. This project either (a) grounds the Cancellation rule in a defensible metaphysics and traces its consequences carefully, or (b) shows the rule is one of several viable choices and characterizes the alternatives.

## Research Questions (from Handoff)

1. **RQ1:** What does "crossing" ontologically entail? Is the mark an instruction, a state, or both?
2. **RQ2:** Characterize the void — "staying put" vs. "nothingness"
3. **RQ3:** Does cancellation imply boundaries don't exist? When does a boundary persist vs. cancel?
4. **RQ4:** Is "boundary = measurement" (the M-property) justified?
5. **RQ5:** Are the (C, X, D) reduction rules confluent under all interpretations?
6. **RQX:** What alternative reduction systems exist?
